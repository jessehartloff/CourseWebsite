== Overview

In the current tech industry you will most likely be interacting with multiple communities of software engineers. It is no longer sufficient for a professional software engineer to simply write great code. They also need to communicate with other engineers about their code, agree on interfaces, understand requirements and use cases, work with outside contractors, and present their work to both technical and non-technical audiences.

Up to 20 points will be awarded for interacting with technical individuals and communities. There are a large variety of ways to earn points outlined below allowing you to choose your own adventure on your path to 20 points. It expected that all students will be able to easily earn the full 20 points as long as appropriate effort is put forth. Any tasks completed as a team may have the points divided among the team members (these teams can be different from your group for the course project).

Many of the points in this section can be awarded retroactively. If you have been actively working with software communities you may already have points from this section.


Much of the grading in this section will be subjective and will vary greatly due to the large amount of flexibility offered. To avoid frustration, ask any grading questions you may have before completing a task.

#} == Setup Content Creation and Submission (5 Points + 1 Point per Submission; max 10 Points Total)

Example: Setup a level creator for a game.

Setup the infrastructure for others to create content for your group project, or another project. The content does not have to be technical. The process to create content must be appropriately documented such that a developer can create and submit content without asking the group for clarification. Content must be submitted via a pull request if it will be added to the repository. Note that you don't necessarily have to create tools to assist the generation of the content, but you must clearly define how to create content that can be used by the project. This can be done by defining an interface, a protocol, or a spec and making the details available for potential content creators.

You must also provide support for anyone submitting content and make sure any submitted content is incorporated into the project. You will be awarded 1 additional point each time content is added to the project in this way with a maximum of 5 addition points for a total maximum of 10 points for setting up content creation and incorporating submissions.

Note that within the group project no credit will given for completing non-technical tasks. If your project requires non-technical work, such as modeling or manual data collection, this is an opportunity to crowd source this work and improve the quality of the project while earning credit.

This cannot be counted for both community credit here if it is used for individual task credit within the group (No double dipping on this one).

== Create Content for Other Groups (2 Points Each; max 10 Points)

Provide content for any project with a content creator setup. Content must be submitted with a pull request if it will be added to the teams repository. Multiple submissions may be made for the same project (Example: Creating 5 levels for a single game project will result in 10 points being awarded).

No credit will be given for creating content for your own project.

== Contribute to a Group Project (5 Points)

Complete a task for another group project from this course (not your own group). The project may be from a previous semester, but must have originated as a CSE442 project. This must not be your own group and the task must have at least the same level of involvement as an individual task assigned for a sprint. A pull request must be submitted with the completed feature for these points to be awarded. These points may be awarded even if the pull request was denied as long as the reason for denial was not related to the quality of the submitted code. For example, if the request is denied because the dev team decided they didn't want your feature in the final product these points can still be awarded. Judgement calls will be made by the course staff.

== Contribute to an Open Source Project (10 Points)

Contribute to an open-source software project that is not associated with this course, past or present, and submit your changes with a pull request. The contribution must have at least the same level of involvement as an individual task assigned for a sprint on the in-class project.

== Hackathon Participation (5 Points)

Compete in an approved hackathon. Actually compete, don’t just register.

== Present to the Public (5 Points)

Present a software project, not necessarily your group project, at an approved public event. This includes poster presentations.

Approved event will be announced on Slack. If you are interested in an event that has not been approved, please notify the course staff for consideration.

== Side Project (10 Points)

Complete, deploy, and post a YouTube video for a project other than your group project. The scope of this project can be less than the group project, but must be substantial enough to appear on your resume. If the project doesn't appear on your resume, it is not substantial enough for credit here. The project must also meet the same deployment and final video requirements as the group project for this course. Side projects may be completed in groups as long as the scope increases proportionately with the group size.

These points can be awarded retroactively as long as the project meets all the criteria. Work completed for credit in other courses will not be considered for credit.


There is a significant amount of ambiguity as to whether or not a project can count for these points. Please seek approval from the course staff before starting a new project for these points. Approval decisions will be made on a case-by-case basis and will depend primarily on the scope of the project.
== Switching Groups (2 Points)

Voluntarily switch groups between sprints. For these points to be awarded both groups must unanimously support the change, the switch must not be caused by a conflict of any kind, and there must not be any strange circumstances surrounding the switch. You may still switch groups under some of these conditions, but no points will be awarded for the switch.

The intent of rewarding such a switch is to encourage you to work with a wider variety of developers and allow you to explore more code bases. You could even switch at the end of each sprint to effectively act as a contractor for the class, as long as all affected groups and group members are compliant. If this intent is broken in any way, no points will be awarded.

== Academic Community (max 10 Points)

A maximum of 10 points can be earned for behaviour that fosters a sense of community within the academic environment at UB.

= CSE442 Community (Points Vary)

Perform any task that benefits the class or course as a whole. This can include setting up servers/machines for the lab, coordinating a guest speaker from industry, developing tools to be used by classmates, etc.

Due to the wide variety of tasks that can fall under this category, point values will be determined by the course instructor on a case-by-case basis.

The intent of these points is to reward students who promote a sense of community within the course. Please do not ask the course staff for ways to earn points from this section. That would be completely against the spirit of these points. Instead, try asking your classmates for ideas.

= Course Community (Points Vary)

Contribute in a meaningful way to a STEM course other than CSE442. This may include generating content, testing homework, providing feedback, guest speaking, answering questions on a course Piazza, providing help to incoming freshmen, building infrastructure, etc.

Due to the wide variety of tasks that can fall under this category, point values will be determined by the course instructor on a case-by-case basis.

The intent of these points is to reward students who promote a sense of community at UB. Please do not ask the course staff for ways to earn points from this section. That would be completely against the spirit of these points. Instead, try asking your fellow students for ideas.

= Working for Courses (5 Points; max 5 Points)

Contribute to a technical course by volunteering as a ninja or working as a TA. Points can be awarded for work in a previous semester.

== Technical YouTube Video (5 Points Each; max 10 Points)

Create and upload a YouTube video on a technical topic. Possibilities include a tutorial on a technical tool, a tutorial on specific coding concepts, advice to future students of this course, advice to future STEM students, etc.

== Contribute to Wikipedia (1-4 Points Each; max 10 Points)

Contribute to a Wikipedia article related to software. The contribution must follow all of Wikipedia's policies. If you contribution is removed for any violation, these points will also be removed.

Point values will be based on the magnitude of the contribution.

== Research (5 Points)

Work with a technical faculty member on a research level project. More points may be awarded depending on the contributions made to the project. The first 5 points will be awarded for attending the research meetings and getting up to speed on the progress already made. To earn these points you must be able to describe the technical aspects of the project and answer questions about the research to verify that you understand the problem and approach.
