--- title: Syllabus --- == Academic Integrity
CSE Department Academic Integrity Policy: http://www.cse.buffalo.edu/undergrad/policy_academic.php

Course Academic Integrity Policy: Do not represent work as your own if you did not create it.

All violations will be result in: 

An F in CSE442.


It is your responsibility to understand what constitutes an academic integrity violation. If you have any question whether something you are doing is a violation or not, ask for clarification before receiving an F in the course. I will not entertain excuses after you have been caught.

Examples of acceptable behavior:
  • Searching the Internet for coding help and examples.
  • Using libraries in your project.
  • Expanding an existing project as your course project.
Examples of unacceptable behavior:
  • Copying a large amount of code into your project without crediting the source.
  • Allowing someone outside your group to write code for your project and representing it as the groups work.
  • Violating the licensing agreement of publicly available code.
  • Breaking laws.
== Course Structure

This course is focused on producing and deploying functional software and will focus on a semester-long group project. The course will take place in a lab environment while you work on your project as opposed to a series of traditional lectures.

Group Project (50 Points)

The course will primarily consist of a semester-long group project.

  • Sprint 1 Progress
  • Sprint 2 Progress
  • Sprint 2 Video Submission
  • Sprint 3 Progress
  • Sprint 4 Progress
  • Sprint 4 Video Submission
  • Presentation
  • Deployment

Sprint Progress will be assessed by the groups productivity and professionalism throughout the sprint. To earn full credit the group must make progress on the project, have a deliverable to show the customer, attend meetings and use acceptable software engineering practices including proper branching and task tracking with a scrum board.

Sprint Videos are short demonstrations of your software that are posted to YouTube. These videos should be made to appeal to a wide audience and should not be very technical nor should they show any code. The specific nature of these videos are largely dependent on the specific project and should be appropriate for your software. Examples of videos can be found on the project page from the previous version of this course http://ub-cse.github.io/CSE442-Fall2015-Projects.html.

Presentations are live demonstrations of your project to the class. These presentations can include a demonstration of the software as well as a technical description of how it works.

Deployment means that by the end of the semester your project must be made publicly available to anyone interested in using it. Note that this does not imply that you must release the source code for your project and you are free to monetize your project. If you choose to monetize, interested investors and/or customers must have a way to give you money by the end of the semester to earn full credit for deployment.

Examples of what can be accepted for deployment if your group chooses to monetize include:

  • Release your software for sale on a publicly available platform
  • Start a crowd sourced project
  • Deliver a VC pitch to potential investors

Examples of what can be accepted for deployment if your group chooses not to monetize include:

  • Release your software for free on a publicly available platform
  • Host your source code in a public repository with clear and accurate documentation installation instructions
  • Submit a research paper based on results produced by your software to a research conference or journal
  • Build and deliver finished software to a non-paying customer who has been working with the group throughout the development process

== Course Description

This course will help bridge the gap between academia and industry through the experience of a semester long group software project. Students will learn a variety of software engineering concepts and realities while functioning as a software development team. Students will also learn the importance of continual career development through self-learning.
